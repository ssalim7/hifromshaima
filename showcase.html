Project 1 ¬∑ Nori & Ember Restaurant Concept

Japanese-inspired restaurant concept near Salford Quays

Role: Concept designer and business analyst

In this project, I developed Nori & Ember as a restaurant concept designed for students, young professionals and local residents. I treated it as a small business case, thinking about the brand, customer experience, and the role of digital tools.

Problem I addressed

  • Independent restaurants can struggle to stand out online against large chains.
  • Customers often want to see location, food style, and atmosphere before they decide to visit.
  • Without a clear digital presence, it is harder to build awareness and customer trust.

Approach I took

  • Researched Salford Quays / MediaCity as a location with a mix of offices, students and visitors.
  • Defined a target audience and a clear food identity (bowls, skewers, small plates).
  • Created a simple visual concept using interior imagery and food photos.
  • Planned how data (for example bookings or feedback) could be used to improve decision making later.

Outcome

  • A digital concept that clearly communicates what Nori & Ember offers and who it is for.
  • An understanding of how location, visuals and copy work together to attract customers.
  • A base I can develop further with detailed financials, menus or loyalty ideas in future modules.

Visuals & location

To make the concept feel realistic, I combined an interior moodboard, a map, and food visuals.

The images subtly highlight when interacted with, hinting at how the full site could respond to user actions.

Project 2 ¬∑ Problem-Solving Page Design Concept

Digital transformation case study

Role: Page designer, content writer and front-end developer

Explaining a digital transformation case clearly

The Problem-Solving page was a project where I turned the assignment brief into a digital case study for Nori & Ember. I had to explain the restaurant‚Äôs digital transformation goals, show contact and social channels, and design an interactive form that could generate an email message.

Problem I addressed

  • The brief required all digital transformation information to sit on one page without confusing the reader.
  • I needed to balance business content (context, goals, challenges) with practical web features (form and integration).
  • The page had to demonstrate HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript working together in a realistic way.

Design concept & structure

  • I opened with a short introduction explaining the restaurant and its digital goals, so readers quickly understand the context.
  • I used separate cards/sections for business context, digital challenges and goals to keep the information skimmable.
  • I added clear contact details and social media links to represent a modern hospitality business.
  • I designed an interactive form that uses JavaScript to build a mailto: link, turning user input into a ready-to-send email.
  • I included a small reflection area at the end of the page, linking the design choices back to digital transformation and my degree.

Outcome

  • A structured case study page that follows the brief and reads like a professional digital proposal.
  • A working example of how simple JavaScript can support bookings and feedback without a full database.
  • A project that clearly links Business Management with Innovation & Technology to a realistic scenario.
