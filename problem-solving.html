Digital problem-solving for Nori & Ember

Nori & Ember is a Japanese-inspired restaurant concept I created for my Business Management with Innovation & Technology coursework. I treated it as a real business case and asked: how could a small, independent restaurant in Salford use digital tools to improve customer engagement, modernise communication, and make bookings less stressful for both staff and guests?

This page shows how I used HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript to design simple, realistic digital solutions for Nori & Ember‚Äôs website ‚Äî from contact and social media integration to an interactive booking and feedback form.

The digital experience is designed to match the physical space: warm, modern, and welcoming, with clear information and low stress for visitors.

Business context

Why Salford?

Salford Quays and MediaCity bring together students, young professionals, and local residents. Most of these customers are very digital: they check maps, Instagram, and online menus before choosing where to eat.

For a new restaurant like Nori & Ember, a clear, responsive website is essential for standing out against bigger chains and for showing exactly what the restaurant offers before customers even walk in.

Digital challenges identified

  • Customers need to quickly understand the concept, menu style, and price point.
  • Bookings and feedback can easily get lost or be difficult to track if done manually.
  • Students and young professionals often judge restaurants by their online presence first.
  • Social media engagement is important but messy without a clear website to link back to.
  • Small teams cannot answer questions 24/7, even though customers expect instant responses.

Digital transformation goals

What the website needs to achieve

  • Improve customer engagement with clear visuals, simple sections, and an easy-to-read layout.
  • Modernise communication through integrated social links, a clear contact panel, and consistent brand voice.
  • Streamline bookings and feedback using an interactive form that automatically generates an email.
  • Build an online presence that connects the website with Instagram, TikTok, and X for ongoing engagement.
  • Show smart use of technology (HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and optional AI support) to solve practical business problems.
